🔥 Most Relatable
I built my entire portfolio with nothing but <div> tags #001

My first portfolio site had 47 nested divs. Forty-seven. The whole page was div > div > div > div like some kind of HTML turducken. I didn't know <main> existed. I didn't know <section> was a thing.

I was out here writing <div class="footer"> like <footer> wasn't right there, free, waiting for me. The day I learned semantic HTML I refactored the whole thing and deleted 30 divs. It felt like cleaning out a closet. 🧹

// lesson_learned.txt
"Every <div> where a semantic element would work is a door with no label. Build the signs."
#html #semantics #divs #beginner-life

😭 The Struggle
I tried to center a div for 3 hours before Googling it #002

Three hours. I tried margin: auto. I tried text-align: center. I tried position: absolute; left: 50% without the transform. I tried width: 50%; margin-left: 25% which almost worked and somehow made everything worse.

I genuinely thought I was bad at math. When I finally Googled it and learned that display: flex; justify-content: center; align-items: center just... works like that... I sat back in my chair and stared at the ceiling for five full minutes.

// lesson_learned.txt
"Google is not cheating. It's the job. Every senior dev you admire Googled how to center a div."
#css #flexbox #centering #we-have-all-been-here
— Jenna 💜

😅 Too Real
I left console.logs in my portfolio during an interview #003

The interviewer opened DevTools to look at my code. I watched them notice console.log("WHY IS THIS STILL BROKEN") buried in my main.js. Then below it: console.log("ok it works now but I don't know why").

There was a pause. Then they laughed. Actually laughed. And said, "This is the most honest portfolio I've seen." I didn't get the job, but I did get feedback that my code was solid. And I learned to always Ctrl+F for console.log before submitting anything. Ever.

// lesson_learned.txt
"Run a find-all for console.log before you ship. Every. Single. Time."
#javascript #debugging #console-log #interviews
— Jenna 💜

💜 From the Heart
I almost quit coding because I felt like I was the only one who didn't "get it" #004

There was a specific Tuesday. I had been staring at a JavaScript error for two days. I'd watched three YouTube tutorials. I'd read the docs. I'd asked on Stack Overflow and gotten a response that made me feel stupid for asking. I closed my laptop and genuinely thought: maybe this isn't for me.

What I didn't know then is that everybody has that Tuesday. Senior developers have that Tuesday. The difference isn't that some people never struggle — it's that some people learned to keep going anyway. I'm still here. That's the whole thing.

// lesson_learned.txt
"The developers who look effortless have just had more Tuesdays than you. That's it. That's the whole secret."
#imposter-syndrome #mental-health #keep-going #you-belong-here
— Jenna 💜

😬 Git Happens
I force-pushed to main and overwrote a week of work #005

I did not understand what git push --force meant. I thought "force" just meant "push even if it's slow." I ran it on a Thursday afternoon. By Thursday evening I had learned exactly what it means.

A week of progress. Gone. I had no local backup. I cried a little. Then I spent the weekend rewriting everything from memory, and honestly? The second version was better. But I will never, not once, run --force without a full breath and a triple-check ever again.

// lesson_learned.txt
"Commit often. Push to branches. Read the docs on any flag that includes the word 'force'."
#git #version-control #rip-my-code #it-happens
— Jenna 💜
